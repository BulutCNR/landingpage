The uncomfortable truth

You wouldn't know
if you'd been breached.

The average breach goes undetected for 207 days. By the time anyone notices, the damage is done — customer data gone, trust destroyed, regulators circling.

Security isn't something you bolt on after launch. But that's exactly what most businesses do. They build fast, they ship, and they tell themselves "we'll deal with security later." Later usually means after the incident.

CNRCO exists for the businesses who decide to act before that happens. Before the call from a customer saying their data was posted online. Before the GDPR fine. Before the news article with your company name in it.

The service

We look for everything
an attacker would.

A security audit isn't a scan you run and forget. It's a methodical investigation of how your system behaves under pressure — what it reveals, what it allows, and what it hides. CNRCO runs the same techniques a real attacker would use, documents every finding with proof, and tells you what to fix first.

PHASE 01 // RECON

We map everything you've exposed before writing a single line of test code.

Before touching your application, we build a full picture of your external attack surface. DNS, subdomains, SSL configuration, email security, open ports, server fingerprints — the kind of reconnaissance a serious attacker would run on day one.

  • DNS & subdomain enumeration
  • SSL/TLS cipher and certificate review
  • HTTP headers, stack fingerprinting
  • SPF, DKIM, DMARC configuration
  • Open port and service discovery

PHASE 02 // TESTING

Automated scans plus manual testing that scanners will never catch.

Tools find the obvious. We find the rest. OWASP Top 10 coverage is table stakes — we go further with manual logic testing: authentication bypass attempts, broken access control, insecure API endpoints, secrets buried in your .git history, and more.

  • SQL injection — manual payloads, not just templates
  • XSS — stored, reflected, DOM-based
  • Broken access control & IDOR
  • API security & rate limiting
  • Hardcoded secrets, exposed .env files

PHASE 03 // REPORT

Every finding scored, prioritized, and explained in plain language.

You don't just get a list of vulnerabilities. You get a CVSS v3.1 scored report with business impact context, proof of concept for every finding, a compliance checklist (GDPR, OWASP, PCI-DSS), and a prioritized remediation roadmap with effort estimates your dev team can actually use.

  • CVSS v3.1 scoring per finding
  • Proof of concept — how we found it
  • GDPR, PCI-DSS, ISO 27001 mapping
  • Remediation steps with code examples
  • Named auditor sign-off

Why this matters

Tools scan.
Professionals think.

There's a real difference between an automated report and an audit. Here's what that difference means for your business.

"A scanner found 12 issues on our site. The auditor found 3 that mattered — including one the scanner completely missed. That one was the critical one."

Accountability

Someone signs their name to it

Automated tools have no liability. If they miss something, no one is responsible. A CNRCO audit is signed by a named auditor. That accountability is part of what you're paying for — and part of what makes the report credible to regulators and insurers.

Context

Judgment, not just pattern matching

A scanner checks if SQL injection templates match. It cannot tell you whether your authentication flow is logically broken, whether your access controls are actually enforced server-side, or whether an insider threat would find a gap in your permission model.

Compliance proof

Regulators don't accept AI output

GDPR Article 32 requires demonstrating "appropriate technical measures." A regulatory body or insurer will not accept an AI-generated checklist as evidence. A signed professional audit report — with named auditor, scope document, and CVSS evidence — will.

Current threats

Trained on now, not last year

AI tools are trained on historical data. They know what was dangerous at training cutoff. A human auditor tracks active CVEs, current exploit chains, and how attackers are actually operating this week — because that's what's coming for your system.

Remediation

A plan, not a dump

Automated tools produce lists. Long, unsorted, context-free lists that overwhelm dev teams and get archived. CNRCO produces a prioritized roadmap with effort estimates, per-item timelines, and code-level remediation steps your team can execute immediately.

Business impact

Your risk, not a generic score

CVSS 7.5 might be critical for a payment processor and trivial for a blog. Business impact ranking translates technical severity into the language of your operations — what would actually happen to your customers, your revenue, and your reputation.
